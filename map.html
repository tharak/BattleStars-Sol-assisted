Successor Stars — strategic map Universe → System → Battle

Real (to-scale, log-compressed for legibility) positions and sizes: bodies sit at their true current orbital angle and distance. The System map is a real 3D (isometric-style, no perspective distortion) view of the Star map and every planet's moons in one -- drag to rotate, right-drag or arrow keys to pan, scroll (zooms toward the cursor) or -/= to zoom manually -- moons are invisible at a glance, same as in real life, so zoom in by hand to see them. Click a breadcrumb (or Zoom Out / Esc) to leave a screen. Nothing on the map carries a floating label -- click a body or a ship and its name shows in the panel at the top right instead. Three factions (Blue, Green, Red) each start with a 12-ship fleet near their home planet. The turn roster at the top left shows which faction is active and which ships remain ready; click any roster entry to focus its ship. Each faction has one minute to activate its living ships before its remaining ships forfeit their movement and play passes to the next faction. Every ship is individually selectable and commandable with the same controls the tactical battle screen uses: Q/E turn, W forward, S back (costs all remaining MP), F arms fire mode (or just click a highlighted enemy in range/arc/LOS to fire directly), Space ends the activation. Ships cannot enter or pass through occupied hexes. Set Course repositions the selected ship on any unoccupied hex in one instant jump, for real interplanetary distances the hex-by-hex MP budget can't cover. Selecting a flagship also reveals its command area and can move every friendly ship inside it as one formation using the same Turn, Forward, and Back controls. The same rules engine also runs standalone as a battle rules test bench, for trying scenarios/fleet sizes/control modes this view doesn't expose.
